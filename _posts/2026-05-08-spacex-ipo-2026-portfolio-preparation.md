#### "Lock-up" Periods

When a company first goes public, there's usually a "lock-up" period. This means that people who owned the company before the IPO (like founders, employees, and big early investors) cannot sell their shares for a certain amount of time, often 90 to 180 days.

Once this lock-up period ends, these early owners are then allowed to sell their shares. If many of them decide to sell at the same time, it can flood the market with shares. This sudden increase in available shares can sometimes push the stock price down.

It's something to be aware of a few months after an IPO. It's a normal part of the process, but it can create temporary pressure on the stock price. This is another reason why patience is a key `spacex investing tip`.
